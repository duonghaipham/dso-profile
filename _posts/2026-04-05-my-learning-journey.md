Software engineering is one of those fields where the learning never stops — and that's exactly what drew me to it.

## Where It Started

My interest in programming started with curiosity: how do websites work? How does software make decisions? Those questions led to my first lines of code.

## What I've Learned Along the Way

The most important lessons weren't about syntax or frameworks. They were:

- **Read the source.** When documentation is unclear, the code is the truth.
- **Break things intentionally.** You learn more from a controlled failure than from everything working on the first try.
- **Teach what you learn.** Writing or talking about a concept is the best test of understanding.

## What's Next

I'm continuing to deepen my understanding of distributed systems, improve my Go and systems programming skills, and contribute more to open source.

This blog is part of that journey — a record of what I'm learning and thinking.
